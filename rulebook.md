26 May – 5 June 2026Finale 3 July 2026, Flipkart HQ Bengaluru ₹5,00,000 prize pool What you're solving
Bengaluru is one of India's most ambitious cities. Getting around in it shouldn't be a daily compromise. Gridlock Hackathon 2.0 is Flipkart's call to India's sharpest AI/ML minds, to build real solutions, on real data, with a real shot at going live on Bengaluru's roads. Chronic traffic congestion in a city of over 14 million people continues to affect economic activity, productivity, and quality of life — and the problem is only growing.
CCTV cameras are deployed across the city to monitor density, congestion, violations and safety concerns like helmet usage, but most analysis still depends on manual review, limiting speed, accuracy and scale. Gridlock 2.0 is Flipkart's call to India's problem-solvers to design AI models that classify congestion, detect violations, identify movement patterns, and support smarter mobility decisions, built on real data, not simulations.

What is the objective of this hackathon?
To inspire participants to develop innovative AI-driven solutions for Bengaluru's traffic challenges, with a focus on building scalable and practical models for traffic analysis, congestion detection, safety monitoring, and smarter urban mobility systems.
How can I form or join a team? What is the maximum allowed team size?
You can form a team or participate individually through the HackerEarth platform. A team can consist of 1–4 members. You can also use the discussion group on the hackathon homepage to find team members.

After the prototype phase, the top ten teams will be invited to the Flipkart headquarters in Bengaluru for the final phase. Each team will be required to present their final solution and demonstrate their functional prototype before a panel of experts.
